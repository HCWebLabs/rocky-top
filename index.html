Our Heritage

A Proud Past, A Bright Future

From rebellion to revitalization, Rocky Top's story is one of resilience, courage, and community spirit.

Early 1800s

Coal Creek is Born

Founded after the 1798 Treaty of Tellico. Welsh miners arrive in 1867, establishing a thriving coal mining community.

1891-1892

The Coal Creek War

Miners fought the convict lease system. On Halloween 1891, hundreds of masked miners freed 300 convicts and burned stockades. Tennessee became the first Southern state to end convict leasing in 1893.

1902 & 1911

Mining Tragedies & Reform

The Fraterville Mine Disaster killed 216 men and boys, while Cross Mountain claimed 84 lives. These tragedies led to nationwide mining safety reforms.

1936

Lake City Era

The town becomes Lake City after the TVA completes Norris Dam, creating opportunities for tourism and recreation.

2014

Rocky Top Renaissance

Embracing the famous Tennessee song, the town becomes Rocky Top, launching revitalization focused on tourism and outdoor recreation.

Today

Growing & Thriving

New businesses open regularly, outdoor enthusiasts visit from around the world, and the community spirit lives on stronger than ever.

Outdoor Recreation

Adventure Awaits

From world-class ATV trails to pristine fishing waters, Rocky Top is an outdoor paradise.

Windrock Park

The largest privately-owned off-road recreation area in America! Over 72,000 acres with 300+ miles of trails. Rocky Top is the ONLY place in Tennessee where ATVs can legally ride on highways.

World-Class Fishing

Cove Lake offers 210 acres of prime fishing. Norris Lake features 800 miles of pristine shoreline and 22+ marinas.

Cove Lake State Park

Mountain valley setting with 106 campsites, nature trails, paved bike paths, and Rickards Ridge BBQ on site!

Norris Lake

One of the cleanest lakes in North America with 34,000 acres. Perfect for boating, skiing, kayaking, and swimming.

Hiking & Nature

Explore 11 miles of Cumberland Trail. Great Smoky Mountains National Park is only 30 minutes away.

Gateway Location

Perfect I-75 access, 24 miles to Knoxville. Your ideal basecamp for East Tennessee adventures!

Community

Events & Celebrations

Rocky Top comes alive with community events, festivals, and celebrations throughout the year.

Christmas Parade

Annual tradition bringing the community together each December with floats, music, and holiday cheer.

Coal Miners Reunion

Honoring hardworking miners with guided historical tours, live music, and community gatherings.

Windrock Events

Year-round off-road events and races including the famous Spring Shindig bringing riders nationwide.

Museum Tours

Guided tours of Coal Creek Miners Museum and historical sites including Militia Hill and Fraterville Miners' Circle.

Fishing Tournaments

Competitive fishing events on Norris Lake and Cove Lake throughout the year, drawing anglers from near and far.

Live Music

Local venues feature live music on weekends, including performances at Rickards Ridge BBQ and community gatherings.
